About

Researcher who ships. Engineer who reads papers.

I started in logistics engineering in China, taught computer fundamentals, IoT and Java as a junior lecturer for four years, co-founded a small training school, and then moved to New Zealand in 2021 to retrain as a computer-vision researcher. That mixed background — teacher, founder, engineer, researcher — is now my superpower: I can explain hard ideas to anyone, hold a research roadmap in my head, and still get my hands dirty in PyTorch on a Friday night.

My doctoral work designs frequency-aware spatio-temporal graph networks for skeleton-based human activity recognition; my master’s research on real-time fruit detection and tracking has racked up 50+ citations on a single paper. Outside the lab, I’ve been an AI Developer at TOHU MEDIA — a Māori-owned New Zealand media-production company — for over four years, building robust object-detection and OCR pipelines for film-industry video, the kind of work where the test set is whatever the camera sees tomorrow.

Most recently I’ve been working with generative AI and agentic systems: Claude / GPT integration, tool use, Model Context Protocol (MCP), and skill-based agents — the next chapter of computer-vision deployment, in my view, is models that act, not just predict.

“Models live in the lab. Systems live in the world. I want to build the second one.” — guiding line for the next phase
Research

Three threads of work.

My research sits between deep learning, computer vision, and structured time-series — with one eye always on whether the model would survive contact with real footage.

i.

Skeleton-based HAR

Spatio-temporal graph convolutional networks with frequency-aware topology learning and dynamic self-attention gating. Published in Pattern Recognition (2026, Q1) and ICONIP (2024).

ii.

Real-time detection & tracking

Improved YOLO architectures and multi-object tracking pipelines for agricultural video — kiwifruit detection / counting. Lead paper sits at 50+ citations.

iii.

Applied CV in industry

Production object-detection and OCR systems for film-industry video at TOHU MEDIA — latency, robustness, and the messy realities of real footage.

Projects

Things I've built.

A selection of research projects and industry work, with the technical stack at the bottom.

PhD · 2024–2026

FATL-GCN — Frequency-Aware Spatio-Temporal Topology Learning

A graph-convolutional architecture for skeleton-based human activity recognition that learns topology jointly in the spatial and frequency domains.

  • Outperforms prior SOTA on NTU RGB+D 60 / 120 benchmarks.
  • Accepted at Pattern Recognition (Q1 journal, IF ≈ 7).

PhD · 2023–2024

Dynamic Self-Attention Gated ST-GCN

Spatial-temporal GCN with a learned gating mechanism that decides how much spatial vs. temporal context to use per joint, per frame.

  • Presented at ICONIP 2024.
  • Improves robustness on cross-subject and cross-view splits.
